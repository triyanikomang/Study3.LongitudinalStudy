# Study3.LongitudinalStudy
Objective: To establish a longitudinal study investigating the interrelationship of dietary intake, gut microbiome, and growth in early adolescents (8 – 13 years old).

This study will follow MiGrowD 8 – 9 years old children longitudinally at 3 time points, 8 – 9 years, 10 – 11 years, and 12 – 13 years, with collections of stool, dietary recall (ASA24), anthropometrics, pubertal assessment questionnaire, and blood collection at the last time point. 

This longitudinal design will allow for the identification of critical periods of change, stable or fluctuating microbiome signatures, and their predictive value for growth outcomes. 

Incorporating the feasibility findings from Study 1 and significant predictors in Study 2 will support the development of effective strategies for conducting microbiome research among early adolescents in large-scale populations.
